Built for India's Defence Ecosystem

WYNTIQ is developed in alignment with India's national defence innovation framework — iDEX, ADITI, Make in India, Atmanirbhar Bharat, and the Defence Acquisition Procedure. Here is how we fit into India's mission to build a self-reliant defence technology sector.

India's Defence Innovation Ecosystem
The Framework We Are Part Of

India's Ministry of Defence has created a comprehensive ecosystem to develop indigenous defence technology — funding Indian startups, facilitating evaluation by the Armed Forces, and creating procurement pathways for Indian-built solutions. WYNTIQ is positioned within this ecosystem at every level — from the product's design to the company's registration and procurement eligibility.

🛡️
iDEX
Innovations for Defence Excellence

iDEX is a Ministry of Defence initiative launched in 2018 under the Defence Innovation Organisation (DIO). It supports Indian startups, MSMEs, and innovators in developing technology that directly addresses Indian Armed Forces requirements. iDEX operates through the DISC (Defence India Startup Challenge) programme, which issues challenge statements from the Services and funds selected solutions through a competitive evaluation process.

🏛️ Ministry of Defence, Government of India
💰 Up to ₹1.5 crore funding per DISC selection
🎯 Problem statements from IAF, Army, Navy
WYNTIQ's alignment: WYNTIQ addresses iDEX problem statements in integrated material management, digital logistics, and offline-capable field management systems. TwinStance Solutions is actively pursuing DISC evaluation for WYNTIQ's core capabilities.
🚀
ADITI
Acing Development of Innovative Technologies with iDEX

ADITI is a scheme under iDEX for deep technology innovation in defence, offering funding up to ₹25 crore for selected projects. ADITI focuses on high-priority technology areas identified by the Indian Armed Forces — including logistics management, supply chain digitalisation, and field-deployable management systems. ADITI projects receive both substantial funding and direct facilitation of evaluation by the relevant Service Headquarters, creating a pathway from innovation to deployment.

💰 Up to ₹25 crore per selected project
🎯 Deep tech in defence priority areas
🤝 Direct facilitation to Service evaluation
WYNTIQ's alignment: WYNTIQ's AI-powered offline IMMS directly addresses ADITI priority areas in defence logistics technology. TwinStance Solutions is eligible for and engaged with the ADITI application process.
🏭
Make in India
Ministry of Commerce and Industry

Make in India is the Government of India's initiative to develop India's manufacturing sector, attract investment, foster innovation, enhance skill development, protect intellectual property, and build best-in-class manufacturing infrastructure. For defence technology, Make in India directly aligns with the policy objective of reducing import dependence and building a domestic defence industrial base. Software and technology built in India for India's defence sector is a direct embodiment of the Make in India mission.

🇮🇳 WYNTIQ is 100% conceived, designed, and built in India
👥 All employees are Indian, based in India
🏛️ Registered Indian LLP (LLPIN: AAQ-0042)
WYNTIQ's alignment: TwinStance Solutions embodies Make in India completely. Every rupee spent on WYNTIQ stays in India, every job created is an Indian job, and every piece of defence operational data stays on Indian soil.
💪
Atmanirbhar Bharat
Self-Reliant India — Ministry of Defence

Atmanirbhar Bharat in defence means reducing India's dependence on foreign defence equipment, technology, and software — building Indian capability to design, develop, and manufacture the defence products that India needs. The Ministry of Defence has issued positive indigenisation lists, set targets for domestic procurement, and created structural incentives to develop Indian defence technology companies. India currently imports significant defence software from foreign vendors. WYNTIQ directly addresses this dependency in logistics management.

📋 Positive indigenisation list eligibility
🛒 IDDM category in Defence Acquisition Procedure
🔒 Data sovereignty — no foreign data exposure
WYNTIQ's alignment: WYNTIQ is the Atmanirbhar alternative to foreign logistics management software for Indian defence. Built in India, designed for India, data stays in India, supported by Indians. This is what strategic self-reliance looks like in practice.

GeM
Government e-Marketplace

GeM is the Government of India's procurement portal for goods and services by Central Government ministries, departments, public sector undertakings, and autonomous bodies. GeM registration allows government organisations to procure WYNTIQ through the standard government procurement process, significantly simplifying the procurement pathway for government customers and ensuring transparency in pricing and selection.

🛒 Standard government procurement pathway
📋 TwinStance Solutions in GeM registration process
🔎 Transparent, auditable procurement
WYNTIQ's alignment: GeM registration is in progress. Until completion, direct procurement from TwinStance Solutions is available with full documentation support for procurement committees.
🏢
MSME India
Ministry of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises

TwinStance Solutions qualifies as a Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprise under GoI classification criteria. MSME status provides procurement preferences under the Public Procurement Policy for MSMEs, which requires Central Government entities to source minimum 25% of procurement from MSMEs. MSME registration also provides access to various GoI schemes for credit, technology support, and export promotion.

✅ TwinStance Solutions is MSME registered
📋 25% procurement preference for Central Govt entities
💡 MSME documentation available for procurement
WYNTIQ's alignment: Government entities that source from WYNTIQ can count the procurement toward their MSME sourcing obligations. TwinStance Solutions can provide required documentation for procurement preference claims.
Why This Alignment Matters
WYNTIQ is not just a technology product. It is India's answer to a strategic dependency.
🔒
Data Sovereignty
India's defence logistics data — stock positions, maintenance status, operational capabilities — should not be managed by foreign software running on foreign infrastructure governed by foreign law. WYNTIQ keeps this data in India, always.
🛠️
Operational Fit
Foreign logistics software is built for foreign military doctrine. Indian Defence has its own approval hierarchies, documentation requirements, and operational procedures that foreign software can only approximate. WYNTIQ is built for how India's Armed Forces actually work.
🔗
Geopolitical Resilience
Dependence on foreign software creates a vulnerability — access can be restricted during political tension. Indian defence logistics should run on Indian software maintained by Indian people who are subject to Indian jurisdiction. Full stop.
Evaluation Pathway
How Defence Organisations Can Evaluate WYNTIQ

TwinStance Solutions supports formal evaluation through multiple pathways depending on the organisation's requirements and procurement procedures.

1. Initial Demonstration
Contact TwinStance Solutions to request a customised demonstration of WYNTIQ configured for your specific service branch and operational context. Demonstrations can be conducted virtually or in person at a location of your choosing. No commitment is required at this stage.
2. Technical Documentation Review
TwinStance Solutions provides complete technical documentation — system architecture, security architecture, data flows, API documentation, and compliance documentation. This supports the technical evaluation that precedes any formal procurement decision.
3. Structured Pilot Deployment
A structured pilot deployment at one unit or location allows evaluation of WYNTIQ in your actual operational environment, with your real data and your real users. TwinStance Solutions provides full support for pilot deployments, including configuration, training, and dedicated technical support during the pilot period.
4. iDEX / ADITI Formal Evaluation
For evaluations that proceed through the iDEX or ADITI framework, TwinStance Solutions cooperates fully with the evaluation process managed by the Defence Innovation Organisation, including participation in evaluation trials and provision of information requested by evaluating officers.
5. Procurement
Following successful evaluation, procurement can proceed through direct contract with TwinStance Solutions, through GeM (when registration is complete), through iDEX-facilitated procurement, or through other procurement mechanisms as applicable to the organisation's procurement procedures.

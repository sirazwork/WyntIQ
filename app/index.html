Aircraft Grounded. Parts Missing. No Visibility.

IAF currently uses IMMOLS — but it requires internet and lacks real-time field integration. Aircraft at forward bases in Ladakh, Andaman, Northeast India face chronic supply delays because the system doesn't work offline.

The Real Cost of Grounded Aircraft

Every hour an IAF aircraft sits grounded due to a missing part is not just a logistics failure — it is an operational failure. During a border crisis, during a flood relief mission, during a peacekeeping operation — a grounded aircraft can mean lives lost, missions failed, national security compromised.

The Indian Air Force currently operates over 1,700 aircraft across 130+ air bases, air force stations, and advanced landing grounds. Managing spare parts, maintenance schedules, and procurement across this network — largely on paper and phone calls — is the single biggest operational inefficiency in the force.

Why IMMOLS Is Not Enough
IMMOLS (Integrated Materials Management Online System), developed by TCS, was a significant step forward. But it was designed in an era when internet connectivity at forward bases was assumed. Today, forward operating locations in Ladakh, the Andaman & Nicobar Islands, and Northeast India face chronic connectivity challenges. IMMOLS stops working. WYNTIQ keeps going — because it was built offline-first, not retrofitted.

How WYNTIQ Works Inside an IAF Base

Step 1 — Technician Raises Demand Signal
A ground crew technician identifies a faulty fuel pump on AN-32 (KF-304). Opens WYNTIQ on tablet — works offline. Fills in: aircraft ID, work order, part number FP-AN32-007, quantity 2, priority URGENT. Submits. Demand Signal DS-047 created in 90 seconds.

Step 2 — System Checks Store First
WYNTIQ checks store inventory automatically. If 2 units available — issued immediately. No officer needed. Zero delay. If not available — moves to approval chain. In this case — only 1 unit in stock. Partial issue + procurement triggered.

Step 3 — Officer Notified Instantly
Squadron Leader receives push notification on WYNTIQ. Reviews demand, justification, aircraft status. Approves with one click. Time taken: 4 minutes. Previously: 4 hours waiting for paper to reach desk.

Step 4 — Logistics & Accounts
Logistics confirms stock position. Accounts verifies budget allocation — ₹12,500 approved. All digital. All timestamped. No paper. No phone calls. Total time from demand to accounts approval: 2.5 hours. Previous system: 2.5 days.

Step 5 — Vendor PO Auto-Generated
For the second unit not in stock, WYNTIQ automatically generates Purchase Order PO-IAF-2026-0047 to the approved vendor. No manual PO writing. Vendor receives notification. Delivery tracked to gate.

Step 6 — Audit Trail Complete
Every single step — who raised, who approved, who issued, time taken at each stage — permanently logged. Exportable as CSV for MCI audit, CAG audit, or any official inspection. Cannot be modified or deleted.

WYNTIQ for Indian Army

From MT operations to forward base logistics — WYNTIQ digitises Army supply chain management with offline capability for field operations across India's most demanding terrain.

Logistics Is the Backbone of Every Army Operation

The Indian Army operates across some of the world's most extreme terrain — from the glaciers of Siachen at 6,000 metres to tropical jungles of the Northeast to the scorching deserts of Rajasthan. Supply chain management in these conditions is not just difficult — it is a matter of life and death for soldiers in the field.

Army logistics encompasses Mechanical Transport (MT), ammunition, rations, medical supplies, weapons, communication equipment, and engineering materials — all flowing through a hierarchy from Corps to Division to Brigade to Battalion to Company level. Coordinating this manually creates delays, duplication, and dangerous gaps.

The MT Section Problem
The Mechanical Transport section of every Army unit manages a fleet of vehicles — from Stallion trucks to BMP infantry carriers. When a vehicle breaks down in the field, the driver raises a demand for spare parts. That demand travels up the chain — officer, logistics, accounts — often taking days. Meanwhile the vehicle sits idle, reducing the unit's operational capability. WYNTIQ cuts this process from days to hours.
Integrated Material Management for Indian Army
The Indian Army follows the Integrated Materiel Management (IMM) system covering Planning, Procurement, Receipt, Storage, Issue, Disposal, and Accounting. WYNTIQ is designed around these exact categories — not a foreign system adapted to India, but a system built for how the Indian Army actually works.

A Ship Without Parts Is Not a Warship

The Indian Navy operates over 130 vessels — from aircraft carriers to submarines to patrol boats — across three major commands: Western (Mumbai), Eastern (Vishakhapatnam), and Southern (Kochi). Each vessel is a floating factory of systems, each system requiring parts, maintenance, and procurement. Managing this across three commands, multiple depots, and at-sea deployments is an extraordinary logistics challenge.

When a frigate is on patrol in the Indian Ocean and its radar system needs a replacement component, that component must be identified, located, approved, procured, and delivered — potentially to a port the ship will call at in 4 days. WYNTIQ makes this coordination possible in real time.

Offline at Sea — The Critical Requirement
Ships at sea do not have reliable internet connectivity. Existing logistics software becomes useless the moment a vessel leaves port. WYNTIQ's offline-first architecture means it works identically at sea as in port — data syncs automatically when the ship docks or gains satellite connectivity. This is not a nice-to-have. Our Mission

TwinStance Solutions was founded in July 2019 with a mission to build technology that solves real problems for India's most critical sectors. We believe India deserves world-class logistics intelligence built specifically for Indian conditions — not adapted from Western systems that don't understand our terrain, our bureaucracy, or our operational realities.

About WYNTIQ

WYNTIQ is our flagship product — India's first AI-powered, offline-first Integrated Material Management System built specifically for Indian Defence and Enterprise logistics.

iDEX Alignment

WYNTIQ is developed in alignment with India's Innovations for Defence Excellence (iDEX) framework under the Defence Innovation Organisation (DIO). We are eligible for DISC and ADITI grant programs.
